What the data reveals
Real completion rate is 61.2% — not 70%
Our simulator was calibrated to 70% based on general wellness program benchmarks. The actual IEO rate is 61.2% — all simulator defaults have been updated to reflect this. This means the 500K target requires either more enrollments, or meaningfully improving completion rates.
25.7% of enrolled never completed a step
13,994 paid and enrolled students let their access expire without completing anything. This is the single most recoverable pool of participants — a targeted re-engagement campaign could potentially unlock 5,000–8,000 additional completions from people already in the system.
Steps 4 and 6 are the critical drop-off points
The funnel loses 40% between Steps 3 and 4, and 58% between Steps 5 and 6. These two transitions need dedicated nudge campaigns — automated emails, community check-ins, or live support sessions — to materially improve the 61.2% completion rate.
City-level findings
Toronto leads NA with 4,619 enrolled and 65% completion
Toronto is the #1 city center by both volume and completion quality. Its combination of active in-person Hatha Yoga calendar and strong online IE pipeline is the model for every other city to replicate.
Chicago has 1,429 online students — but zero in-person programs
Chicago is a top-10 IEO city with nearly 1,500 enrolled students and 58% completion. Yet the in-person calendar is completely empty. Adding even 2 IE cohorts per month in Chicago would serve an existing, proven audience.
LA has the worst completion rate among major cities: 50.1%
With 1,257 enrolled, Los Angeles is underperforming every other major city on completion. The Isha Yoga Center LA provides the physical infrastructure — the gap suggests a support and community engagement problem, not a reach problem.
West Palm Beach: 46.8% — lowest in the entire leaderboard
848 enrolled, only 397 completed. This needs direct investigation — either the audience quality differs, or Step 7 scheduling in this region is a barrier.

Strategic insights, gap analysis & IEO data findings
From the IEO live tracker
Real completion rate: 61.2% — recalibrates the entire model
At 61.2%, reaching 500K completions requires ~817K total enrollments (not the ~714K assumed at 70%). This is a meaningful difference — roughly 103,000 additional enrollments needed to hit the same 500K completion target.
13,994 expired students = the fastest re-engagement opportunity
25.7% of all enrolled never completed any steps. A targeted re-engagement campaign for this group — already inside the system, already paid — could realistically convert 30–50% of them, adding 4,000–7,000 completions at near-zero acquisition cost.
Step 4 is where 40% of active students drop off
The steepest single-step drop in the funnel is Step 3 → Step 4. A dedicated nudge system (automated emails, WhatsApp reminders, satsang invitations) at Step 3 completion could meaningfully close this gap and lift the overall 61.2% rate.
LA: 1,257 enrolled, only 50.1% completion
The worst completion rate among major cities. With the Isha Yoga Center LA as a physical anchor, the reach isn't the problem — completion support and community is. Local in-person Step 7 sessions and satsangs could directly fix this.
Critical gaps — in-person programs
47 of 60 cities have zero in-person programs scheduled
Only 13 cities are active for in-person programming. Yet all 55 cities on the IEO tracker have online students enrolled — meaning in-person activation would directly serve an existing, paying community in every dormant city.
Chicago: 1,429 IEO students, zero in-person programs
Chicago is a top-10 IEO city with a 58% completion rate. Adding in-person IE and Hatha Yoga programs here directly serves an existing community and would likely boost Chicago's online completion rate too.
Toronto + Philadelphia = the replication model
Toronto leads the IEO leaderboard (4,619 enrolled, 65% completion) and runs 18+ in-person programs. Philadelphia runs 9 in-person programs at a consistent venue. These two cities prove the flywheel: in-person activity drives online completion rates up.
Every dormant city already has IEO students to serve
Nashville has 716 enrolled online. Denver has 605. Sacramento 449. These aren't cold markets — they have active communities waiting for in-person programming. The case for activating each dormant city is stronger than ever.

IE 500K Scale-Up Dashboard

A data and simulation tool to support Isha Foundation's goal of bringing Inner Engineering to 500,000 people across North America.

What's inside
📊 IEO live data
Real enrollment, completion rate (61.2%), step funnel, and city leaderboard from the IEO tracker.
📍 In-person programs
Live program data from all 60 NA city pages, including real venues and dates.
🎯 500K simulator
Tune 15 channels with sliders. Defaults recalibrated to real 61.2% completion rate.
💡 Insights
Gap analysis combining IEO online data with in-person scheduling gaps.
Key assumptions — simulator
  • Completion rate (61.2% default): Set to the real IEO completion rate from the Isha leaderboard tracker (Apr 20, 2026 snapshot). Previously assumed at 70% — the actual rate is 8.8 points lower.
  • Expired re-engagement (ambassador channel): 13,994 students expired without completing. The ambassador slider models re-engaging a portion of this pool at a 40% re-activation rate (conservative).
  • City cohort size (30 people default): Estimated from observable in-person event formats at active Isha city centers. Actual capacity varies by venue and city.
  • Digital reach → trial conversion (2% default): Conservative estimate based on wellness program benchmarks. Sadhguru's strong brand may yield higher rates.
  • Corporate enrollment (50 per partnership): Ranges from 10 for a small startup to 500+ for a Fortune 500. Tune per your actual pipeline.
  • Channel volumes are additive: The model assumes minimal overlap. In practice, actual unique completions may be 10–15% lower than the sum of all channels.
  • Completion rate improvement: Each 1-point lift in the 61.2% rate (e.g. via Step 4 nudge campaigns) adds ~544 completions per 54K cohort enrolled — a high-leverage, low-cost lever.
